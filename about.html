Our Story

Sugarwares was founded with a clear mission: to provide sustainable, eco-friendly packaging solutions that don't compromise on quality or functionality. We recognized the urgent need to reduce plastic waste and environmental pollution in the food service industry.

Based in India, the world's second-largest producer of sugarcane, we have direct access to abundant bagasse - a byproduct of sugar production that was traditionally considered waste. By transforming this material into high-quality, biodegradable products, we're creating value from waste and helping protect our planet.

Today, Sugarwares is proud to be at the forefront of the sustainable packaging movement, serving customers worldwide with innovative solutions that make a difference.

    More from less

    Sugarcane is a crop with high bio-conversion efficiency and can be harvested in a single season. For every tonne of sugarcane stalks that are crushed, about 30 tonnes of bagasse can be obtained. Everything that naturally remains from the crushed sugarcane is used in our production line. We don’t waste, even while managing waste.

    Microwave safe

    Bagasse is thermal resistant and can hold hot food up to 95 degree Celsius or 200F. This means the food in a bagasse container can be heated in a microwave for temperatures up to 200F.

    Strong enough to build!

    Bagasse containers and tableware are just as strong and durable as plastic. They are more durable and firmer than polystyrene containers due to the fibrous texture. In fact, various studies have been conducted to investigate the use of sugarcane bagasse ash as a replacement for cement in ordinary, lightweight, and self-compacting concretes.

    Refrigerate until consumption

    Bagasse packing solutions are refrigerator safe and can be used to store food until consumption without worrying about deterioration of the food quality.

    Water and grease resistant

    Bagasse containers are grease the water resistant. Rest assured, your food will not meet an untimely demise in our containers.

    100% Biodegradable

    Bagasse is 100 percent biodegradable under commercial composting conditions and will break down within 30-90 days. While many paper-based products can be composted too, the advantage of bagasse is that growing it does not have the same environmental impact as paper, which comes from trees.

    0% Plastic

    Bagasse, also known as sugarcane pulp, is a fibrous material left behind in the sugarcane-harvesting process. Among many other uses, it is a sustainable alternative to conventional plastic packaging. We strictly follow the zero plastic policy, and our products do not even carry the plastic lining often found on food packaging.

    Raw Material

    Since bagasse is a byproduct of the sugarcane industry, reusing it in the paper-making process reduces the amount of waste produced by sugar mills. Being able to reuse this material to create paper products versus harvesting new materials for paper pulp also reduces deforestation, rendering the paper-making process more eco-friendly.

    Easier Bleaching

    Bagasse is easier to bleach than the normal wood fibers typically used in the paper-making process. Therefore, it requires less chlorine to achieve the bright white colour. After the cooking process, bagasse turns into black pulp. The pulp then goes through washing process three times to change its color. The process for making wood pulp white for paper creation is more complex and uses more chemicals, making it have a larger negative environmental impact.
